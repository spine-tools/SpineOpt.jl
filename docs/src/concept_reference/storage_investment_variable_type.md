Within an investment problem [storage\_investment\_variable\_type](@ref) determines the storage investment decision variable type.
Since a [node](@ref)'s [node\_state](@ref var_node_state) will be limited to the product of the investment variable and the corresponding [storage\_state\_max](@ref) and since [storage\_investment\_count\_max\_cumulative](@ref) represents the upper bound of the storage investment decision variable,
[storage\_investment\_variable\_type](@ref) thus determines what the investment decision represents.

Setting [storage\_investment\_variable\_type](@ref) = `none` disables investment decisions regardless of [storage\_investment\_count\_max\_cumulative](@ref).
If [storage\_investment\_variable\_type](@ref) is integer or binary, then [storage\_investment\_count\_max\_cumulative](@ref) represents the maximum number of discrete storages that may be invested-in. If [storage\_investment\_variable\_type](@ref) is linear (default), [storage\_investment\_count\_max\_cumulative](@ref) is more analogous to a capacity with [storage\_state\_max](@ref) being analogous to a scaling parameter. For example, if [storage\_investment\_variable\_type](@ref) = `integer`, [storage\_investment\_count\_max\_cumulative](@ref) = 4 and [storage\_state\_max](@ref) = 1000 MWh, then the investment decision is how many 1000h MW storages to build. If [storage\_investment\_variable\_type](@ref) = linear, [storage\_investment\_count\_max\_cumulative](@ref) = 1000 and [storage\_state\_max](@ref) = 1 MWh, then the investment decision is how much storage capacity to build. Finally, if [storage\_investment\_variable\_type](@ref) = `integer`, [storage\_investment\_count\_max\_cumulative](@ref) = 10 and [storage\_state\_max](@ref) = 100 MWh, then the investment decision is how many 100MWh storage blocks to build.

See also [Investment Optimization](@ref) and [storage\_investment\_count\_max\_cumulative](@ref).
